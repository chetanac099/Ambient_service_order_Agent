[0:00 — HOOK]
Welcome to the Ambient Service Order Agent demo. In manufacturing, manual review of spare part orders causes expensive downtime, but blanket auto-approvals invite fraud and massive overspending.

[0:20 — WHAT IT IS]
This agent solves that problem. It's a deterministic and generative workflow built with the Google Agent Development Kit (ADK) that auto-approves routine parts while keeping human reviewers tightly in the loop for high-risk or high-cost incidents. 

[0:40 — SHOW THE BANNER]
(show assets/cover_page_banner.png)
At a glance, it's an automated, secure, and intelligent layer between incoming service requests and the final financial authorization.

[1:00 — ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Here's how the workflow moves step-by-step. 
First, the `extract_incident` node parses the incoming request. 
Then it immediately hits the `security_checkpoint`—this is crucial. Before the LLM ever sees the data, we scrub PII and look for prompt injections. 
From there, clean low-cost orders route straight to `auto_approve`. 
If the cost is high, it goes to `llm_review` where the model assesses the risk. 
Crucially, the LLM cannot approve the order itself—it routes to a `human_review` node. 
Only after a human signs off does it reach `create_service_order` or `reject_order`.

This deterministic `security_checkpoint` ensures our LLM is never exposed to PII or malicious instructions. We also designed the `llm_review` node to be extensible with MCP Server tools, allowing it to connect to real-time inventory databases in the future.
